FRM Program Overview

üîç What is FRM All About?

FRM stands for Financial Risk Manager, a globally recognized certification awarded by the Global Association of Risk Professionals (GARP). It is considered the gold standard for professionals working in risk management, investment management, corporate finance, and banking.

The FRM program focuses on identifying, analyzing, and managing risk across various areas of finance. It covers:

  • Quantitative Analysis
  • Financial Markets & Products
  • Valuation & Risk Models
  • Credit, Market, and Operational Risk
  • Liquidity & Treasury Risk
  • Risk Management in Investment Management
  • Current Issues in Financial Markets

The FRM program is divided into two parts and typically takes 1‚Äì1.5 years to complete.

üíº How FRM Helps in a Finance Career

  • Specializes Your Profile in Risk Management: Companies need professionals who can manage financial risk, not just generate returns. FRM helps you assess credit risk, market volatility, interest rate changes, and operational failures.
  • High Demand Across Industries: Especially valued in banks, investment firms, credit rating agencies, insurance companies, and regulatory bodies (e.g., RBI, SEBI).
  • Roles You Can Target: Risk Analyst, Credit Risk Manager, Market Risk Manager, Risk Consultant, Chief Risk Officer (with experience), Regulatory or Compliance Analyst.
  • Complementary to CFA/MBA: CFA is broader (investment focus), while FRM goes deeper into risk. Holding both gives a strong edge in finance careers.
  • Global Recognition: Accepted in 190+ countries and highly preferred by global banks and consulting firms.
  • Prepares You for Real-World Financial Crises: Understand how to respond to situations like the 2008 financial crash and COVID-era volatility.

Professional Experience Requirement: Submit two years of full-time relevant work experience within 10 years of passing FRM Part II. This can be accrued before or after taking the exam.
